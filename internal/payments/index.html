Payments and Ad Spend Handling

This page covers the practical mechanics behind getting paid by clients and handling ad spend on their behalf. The goal is to preserve budget control, auditability, and clean accounting while still giving Benefactor enough flexibility to execute.

Short Answer

Can a client give Benefactor a spend-limited card?

Yes, but the professional version is not a loose physical prepaid debit card. The better answer is a client-controlled virtual card or other controlled funding method with hard limits, reporting, and an agreement that makes the boundaries explicit.

Best practice

The client issues Benefactor a virtual corporate card with spending controls, expiration rules, and a fixed budget ceiling. That card is used only for agreed ad-spend or tool expenses.

  • Hard monthly or campaign-level caps
  • Real-time visibility for the client
  • Ability to shut off immediately if needed
  • Cleaner audit trail than a prepaid debit card

Why not a physical prepaid card?

  • Weaker controls and worse reporting
  • More accounting ambiguity
  • Higher risk if the card is reused or misapplied
  • Less flexibility for setting category-level restrictions
Funding Options

Ways clients can fund spend while controlling budget

Model How It Works Internal Take
Virtual cards The client issues Benefactor controlled virtual cards for ad platforms or approved tools, with hard ceilings and restrictions. Preferred default for ad spend and controlled pass-through costs.
Prepaid retainer drawdown The client prepays a spend pool and Benefactor draws against it under contract-defined limits and approval rules. Workable, but requires tighter internal reporting and trust.
Escrow or managed wallet Funds sit in a managed account and are released based on approvals or milestones. Better for larger or more complex engagements, but slower and heavier operationally.
Reimbursement Benefactor fronts costs and the client reimburses later. Least attractive unless the amounts are small and timing is short.
Recommended Payment Logic

How Benefactor should approach ad spend on behalf of clients

Preferred operating model

  • Client owns the ad account or billing profile whenever possible.
  • Client funds spend directly through a virtual card or billing method they control.
  • Benefactor manages execution, optimization, and reporting on top of that.
  • Any increase above the agreed ceiling requires explicit approval.

If Benefactor must pay first

  • Keep the float small and time-bounded.
  • Use a written reimbursement cap and cadence.
  • Do not let float become an unpriced financing service.
  • Escalate anything that could materially impact agency cash flow.

Default recommendation: pair a contract-defined budget with client-issued virtual cards and written reporting cadence. That gives the client a real-time hard cap and gives Benefactor enough flexibility to execute without taking unnecessary financial risk.
